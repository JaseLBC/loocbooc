## GUIDING PRINCIPLES FOR ALL MESSAGES

- Under 100 words. Readable in under 30 seconds.
- Lead with THEIR background, not Loocbooc

## LINKEDIN STRATEGY NOTES

1. **Connect first, don't cold message** — Connection requests with a short note often get better response rates than InMail. If Jason has mutual connections, those get surfaced by LinkedIn.

2. **Profile check before sending** — Verify current role is as expected. Someone who's just started somewhere new is less likely to be open. Someone 1-2 years in is potentially more open.

3. **Timing** — Monday morning or Thursday afternoon are the best times to send. Avoid Friday afternoon.

4. **Don't mass-send** — These messages should feel handwritten, not templated. Personalise the company name and role specifically. If it reads like a form letter, it won't convert.

5. **The co-founder framing is critical** — Never frame this as "we're hiring a CTO." It's a co-founder conversation. The equity, the ownership, the chance to build something from scratch — that's the entire pitch.

6. **If someone says they're not looking** — Thank them and ask if they know anyone who might be the right fit. Referrals from the right network are often better than cold outreach.
